About the 2026 FIFA World Cup

The 2026 FIFA World Cup is the 23rd edition of the world's most prestigious football tournament, hosted jointly by the United States, Canada, and Mexico. It is the first World Cup to feature an expanded 48-team format, with 16 groups of 3 teams each progressing to a 32-team knockout round. The tournament runs from June 11 to July 19, 2026, culminating in the final at MetLife Stadium in East Rutherford, New Jersey.

All 48 qualified nations are represented with their full 26-man squads, including player profiles (position, club, age, caps, goals) and head coach biographies. Teams are organized by confederation:

  • CONMEBOL (South America) — 6 teams: Argentina, Brazil, Uruguay, Colombia, Ecuador, Venezuela, Paraguay

  • AFC (Asia) — 8 teams: Japan, South Korea, Australia, Iran, Saudi Arabia, Iraq, Jordan, Uzbekistan, Qatar
  • OFC (Oceania) — 1 team: New Zealand

Group Stage Draw

The 48 teams are divided into 16 groups (A–P), with the top two from each group advancing to the Round of 32. Notable groups include Group A (host nation USA), and groups featuring traditional powerhouses such as Argentina, Brazil, France, and Spain.
